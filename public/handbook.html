1. Overview

Wizard of Wor is a 1983 arcade game originally developed by Midway and popularised on the Commodore 64. You control a warrior navigating maze-like dungeons, shooting monsters before they shoot you. The game never ends — dungeons loop forever, growing harder with every level.

This edition is a faithful browser remake of the C64 version, extended with an Endless Connected Dungeon network multiplayer mode where players each inhabit their own dungeon and can invade each other through tunnel connections.

3. Objective

Clear each dungeon of all enemies before they escape or kill you. You earn points for every monster you shoot. The game ends only when all lives are lost.

Every completed dungeon leads to the next, which is harder. Enemies become faster and more aggressive, and the layout changes. There is no final dungeon — the game loops indefinitely.

5. Dungeons

Dungeons are rectangular 11 × 6 tile grids with configurable inner walls. The layout changes each level.

Radar panel

The centre panel at the bottom of the screen is a mini-map showing the position of all visible enemies (invisible ones are also shown). The label above the map changes to reflect the current dungeon name, or special event text.

Double Score dungeon

Killing the Worluk sets a Double Score flag. The next dungeon is played with all point values doubled. The double-score banner is shown at the start of the dungeon.

Bonus life: All living players receive a free life at the start of level 3 and level 12.

6. Speed System

Enemy speed is controlled by two factors: the speed level (1–16) and the background music tempo (1–7). Both increase as you progress through dungeons and as time passes within a dungeon.

Speed increases when

  • Every 25 seconds that pass inside a dungeon
  • Every 4th monster killed in a dungeon
  • The Worluk appears (speed jumps to 14)

8. Tunnels (Teleport Gates)

The left and right walls of the dungeon have teleport gates at row 3 (counting from the top). Walking into a gate teleports you to the other side of the dungeon — or, in multiplayer, to a connected dungeon.

Tunnel rules

  • Tunnels are open at the start of each dungeon.
  • After use, a tunnel closes for a cooldown period (default 13 seconds).
  • Some events force a longer or immediate close: Worluk appearance (5 s), Worluk death (immediately), wizard activity.
  • Most monsters can also use tunnels; the Worluk and Wizard of Wor have special behaviour.

Cross-dungeon transfer (multiplayer)

In multiplayer, each dungeon has a left tunnel target and a right tunnel target — references to other connected dungeons. When you enter a tunnel that is linked to another dungeon, you are transferred to that dungeon and appear at the matching tunnel exit on the opposite side.

Respawn: When you die in a foreign dungeon, you respawn in your own home dungeon — never in the dungeon you were visiting.
Dungeon collapse: When all home players die in a dungeon, it enters Collapsing state. All tunnels are forced open so visitors can escape. After 15 seconds, any remaining visitors are force-killed.

9. Special Events

Worluk sequence

Once you have killed enough Thorwors (the threshold decreases at higher levels), the Worluk appears. The radar displays WORLUK, the wall colour changes, and enemy speed jumps to 14.

  1. Tunnels close for 5 seconds.
  2. Background music switches to the Worluk theme.
  3. Kill the Worluk → Double Score flag set for next dungeon.
  4. Worluk escapes → All living players lose a life; penalty timer begins.

Wizard of Wor sequence

After the Worluk event resolves, there is a 1-in-7 chance the Wizard of Wor spawns:

  1. Speed resets to 12, tunnels open, radar shows WIZARD OF WOR.
  2. The Wizard teleports randomly and fires in all four directions.
  3. Kill the Wizard → Double Score flag is set; dungeon ends.
  4. Wizard shoots a player → Wizard escapes; that player (and all others) lose a life.
Radar warning: When the Wizard is present, watch the radar carefully — it shows his position even after he teleports.

10. Multiplayer — Endless Connected Dungeon

The multiplayer server runs separately from the single-player static server:

npm run multiplayer
# → http://localhost:5001/multiplayer.html

Joining rules

ClickResult
Solo A new dungeon is created just for you. Your dungeon's tunnels are automatically linked to any existing active dungeon. You are assigned the next available warrior colour.
2-Player You wait for a second player to also click 2-Player. Both players share one dungeon with the original WoW side-by-side spawn positions. This is the closest to the classic two-player experience.

Dungeon lifecycle

ACTIVE Normal gameplay. PvP enabled. Tunnels follow normal rules.
↓ All home players lose their final life
COLLAPSING 15-second evacuation window. Tunnels forced open. Visitors force-killed at timeout.
↓ All players evacuated or killed
EMPTY No players. Remaining monsters at ×1.8 speed (fast mode).
↓ All monsters dead or escaped
DESTROYED Dungeon removed. All tunnel links unregistered.

PvP rules

PvP is always enabled. Friendly fire cannot be turned off. Shooting another player earns 1,000 points (×2 during Double Score). There are no safe zones — not even in your home dungeon.

Server authority

The server is fully authoritative. Clients send only raw keyboard state; the server validates all movement, shooting, hits, scoring, tunnel transfers, respawn, and dungeon lifecycle transitions. The client is a pure renderer.

12. Tips & Tricks

Combat

  • You can only have one bullet on-screen at a time — time your shots carefully.
  • Bullets from two players cancel each other out if they collide mid-air.
  • You are briefly invulnerable after spawning — use this window to orient yourself.
  • The Wizard of Wor fires in all four directions; don't stand in a straight line from his position.
  • Invisible Garwors and Thorwors still appear on the radar — always check it.

Score maximisation

  • Always kill the Worluk to trigger Double Score for the next dungeon.
  • The Wizard of Wor is worth 2,500 pts (5,000 during Double Score) — worth the risk.
  • In multiplayer, shooting other players earns 1,000 pts — but leaves you exposed.
  • Killing monsters in groups of 4 triggers a speed-up — use this strategically to control difficulty.

Multiplayer tactics

  • When a dungeon starts collapsing, tunnels open — enemy dungeons become accessible.
  • Fast-mode monsters (×1.8 speed) in abandoned dungeons pose a real threat if you visit without preparation.
  • Entering via a tunnel puts you at row 3. You exit moving right (left tunnel) or left (right tunnel) — use this predictable entry to prepare for combat.
  • Respawn always sends you back to your home dungeon — dying in an enemy dungeon is safer than in your own.
Radar text tells you everything: WORLUK means rush to kill it, WIZARD OF WOR means prepare for chaos, ESCAPED means you lost a life shortly.
